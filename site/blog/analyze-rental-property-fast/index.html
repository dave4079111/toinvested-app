How to Analyze a Rental Property in Under 10 Minutes

By David J. Moore February 10, 2026 8 min read

You don't need hours and a spreadsheet to know if a rental deal is worth pursuing. With the right framework, you can screen properties in under 10 minutes and focus your time on the deals that actually pencil out.

The Quick-Screen Framework

Use these three rules in order — each one takes about 30 seconds and eliminates bad deals quickly:

Filter #1: The 1% Rule (30 seconds)

Monthly rent should be at least 1% of the purchase price

A $200,000 property should rent for at least $2,000/month. Properties meeting the 1% rule typically cash flow well. Below 0.8%, cash flow becomes difficult in most financing scenarios.

1% Rule Quick Screen

$250,000 property renting for $2,500/month: $2,500 ÷ $250,000 = 1.0% ✓ Passes

$350,000 property renting for $2,100/month: $2,100 ÷ $350,000 = 0.6% ✗ Likely won't cash flow

Filter #2: The 50% Rule (30 seconds)

Operating expenses (excluding mortgage) will eat approximately 50% of gross rent

This means if a property rents for $2,000/month, expect about $1,000 to go toward taxes, insurance, maintenance, vacancy, management, and CapEx. The remaining $1,000 covers your mortgage payment — whatever's left is cash flow.

Filter #3: The Cash Flow Test (2 minutes)

Quick Cash Flow Calculation

Monthly rent: $2,000

50% expenses: -$1,000

NOI: $1,000

Mortgage payment (25% down, 7%, 30yr on $187,500): -$1,248

Monthly cash flow: -$248 ✗ Negative — skip this deal

If a deal fails the quick cash flow test, it needs to have exceptional appreciation potential or value-add opportunity to justify pursuing it.

The 5-Minute Deep Dive

If a property passes the quick screen, spend 5 more minutes on a deeper analysis:

Step 1: Verify the Rent (1 minute)

  • Check Zillow Rent Zestimate, Rentometer, or local MLS comps
  • Look at similar active listings in the area
  • Use our AI Property Analyzer for instant rent estimates

Step 2: Itemize Expenses (2 minutes)

  • Property taxes: Look up on county assessor's website
  • Insurance: Budget $80-$150/month for single-family
  • Maintenance: 5% of rent (ongoing repairs)
  • CapEx reserves: 5% of rent (roof, HVAC, water heater)
  • Vacancy: 5-8% (market dependent)
  • Property management: 8-10% if not self-managing

Step 3: Calculate Key Metrics (2 minutes)

  • Cash-on-cash return: Annual cash flow ÷ total cash invested (target 6%+)
  • Cap rate: NOI ÷ purchase price (target 5%+ in most markets)
  • Monthly cash flow: Target $100-$200+ per unit

Deal Killers: Walk Away Immediately If...

  1. Rent-to-price ratio below 0.7% — almost impossible to cash flow
  2. Negative cash flow with no value-add plan — you're speculating on appreciation
  3. Deferred maintenance exceeds 10% of value — unless you're doing a BRRRR deal
  4. Declining population or job market — rent growth needs demand
  5. HOA above $400/month — kills cash flow on most deals
  6. Flood zone without flood insurance budgeted — adds $2,000-$5,000/year

Key Takeaways

  • Use the 1% rule as your first filter — monthly rent should be at least 1% of purchase price
  • The 50% rule estimates that half of gross rent goes to operating expenses
  • Target $100-$200+ per unit in monthly cash flow and 6%+ cash-on-cash return
  • Walk away from deals with sub-0.7% rent ratios, declining markets, or excessive HOA fees
