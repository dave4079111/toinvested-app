BRRRR Strategy Explained: Build Wealth on Repeat

By David J. Moore February 18, 2026 10 min read

The BRRRR strategy is the ultimate capital recycling machine for real estate investors. Instead of tying up $50,000+ in each deal, you recoup most (or all) of your investment through refinancing — then use that capital to buy the next property.

What Is the BRRRR Strategy?

BRRRR stands for Buy, Rehab, Rent, Refinance, Repeat. It combines value-add investing with long-term buy-and-hold, allowing you to build a rental portfolio without needing fresh capital for each property.

Step 1: Buy Below Market Value

Find distressed properties at a significant discount — typically 20-30% below ARV (After Repair Value). Sources include:

  • Off-market deals (direct mail, driving for dollars, wholesalers)
  • Foreclosures and bank-owned properties (REOs)
  • Estate sales and probate properties
  • Properties with deferred maintenance that scare away retail buyers

Finance the purchase with cash, hard money, or private money — you need speed and flexibility.

Step 2: Rehab Strategically

Renovate to maximize the gap between your all-in cost and the ARV. Focus on high-ROI improvements:

  • Kitchens and bathrooms — the biggest value drivers
  • Flooring and paint — cosmetic upgrades with the best cost-to-value ratio
  • Systems (HVAC, roof, plumbing) — required for refinancing appraisal and tenant retention
  • Curb appeal — first impressions matter for both appraisals and tenants

Use our Renovation Analyzer to estimate costs and prioritize improvements.

Step 3: Rent at Market Rate

Place a quality tenant before refinancing. Lenders want to see:

  • Signed lease — at or above market rent
  • Tenant in place — proves the property generates income
  • Rental history — even one month of payments strengthens your refinance application

Step 4: Refinance Based on New Value

This is where the magic happens. Refinance based on the new appraised value (ARV), not your purchase price. With a 75% LTV cash-out refinance, you can pull out most of your original investment.

BRRRR Numbers in Action

Purchase price: $120,000 (distressed, paid cash)

Rehab costs: $35,000

Total invested: $155,000

After Repair Value (ARV): $210,000

Cash-out refinance (75% LTV): $210,000 × 0.75 = $157,500

Capital recovered: $157,500 - closing costs ($3,500) = $154,000

Cash left in deal: $155,000 - $154,000 = $1,000

You now own a $210,000 rental property with only $1,000 out of pocket. Your $154,000 is free to invest in the next BRRRR deal.

Step 5: Repeat

Take your recovered capital and do it again. Theoretically, you could buy unlimited properties with the same pool of capital — each time recycling it through the BRRRR process.

Cash Flow After BRRRR

Ongoing Cash Flow

Monthly rent: $1,650

Monthly mortgage (new loan): -$1,100

Property taxes: -$175

Insurance: -$100

Maintenance/CapEx (10%): -$165

Net monthly cash flow: $110/month ($1,320/year)

With only $1,000 invested, that's a 132% cash-on-cash return.

BRRRR Risks and How to Mitigate Them

  • Appraisal comes in low — leaves more cash in the deal. Mitigate: be conservative on ARV estimates
  • Rehab goes over budget — get multiple contractor bids and add 15% contingency
  • Can't find a tenant — research rental demand before buying
  • Refinance rates increase — run numbers at multiple interest rate scenarios
  • Seasoning requirements — some lenders require 6-12 months before refinancing. Plan your timeline.

BRRRR vs. Traditional Buy and Hold

FactorBRRRRTraditional Buy & Hold
Capital efficiencyRecycle capital (near-infinite ROI)Capital locked in each deal
Scaling speedFast — same capital buys multiple propertiesSlow — need new capital each time
ComplexityHigher — requires rehab and refinance expertiseLower — simpler execution
RiskHigher — rehab + appraisal riskLower — fewer moving parts
Cash flow post-refinanceLower (higher loan balance)Higher (lower or no loan)

Key Takeaways

  • BRRRR lets you recycle capital by refinancing based on the improved property value
  • The goal is to recover 90-100% of your invested capital through cash-out refinance
  • Buy at 70-80% of ARV to ensure the numbers work after renovation
  • Tenant placement before refinancing strengthens your loan application
  • BRRRR trades complexity for capital efficiency — ideal for investors who want to scale fast
