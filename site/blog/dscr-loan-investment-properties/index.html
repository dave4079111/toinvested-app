DSCR Loans for Investment Properties: Complete 2026 Guide

By David J. Moore March 3, 2026 9 min read

DSCR loans have revolutionized real estate investing. Unlike conventional mortgages that scrutinize your W-2 income, DSCR loans qualify you based on the property's income — not yours. This means self-employed investors, full-time investors with no traditional income, and anyone scaling beyond 10 properties can access financing.

What Is a DSCR Loan?

DSCR stands for Debt Service Coverage Ratio. It's a loan product for investment properties where qualification is based on whether the property's rental income covers the mortgage payment. No tax returns, no W-2s, no employment verification.

DSCR = Monthly Gross Rental Income ÷ Monthly PITIA (Principal + Interest + Taxes + Insurance + Association Dues)

A DSCR of 1.0 means the rent exactly covers the mortgage. Above 1.0, there's positive cash flow. Most lenders require a minimum DSCR of 1.0-1.25.

How to Calculate DSCR

DSCR Calculation Example

Property: 3BR/2BA single-family rental

Monthly rent: $2,200

Monthly PITIA: $1,760 (P&I: $1,300 + Taxes: $280 + Insurance: $130 + HOA: $50)

DSCR: $2,200 ÷ $1,760 = 1.25

This property qualifies with most DSCR lenders at a 1.25 ratio.

When to Use a DSCR Loan

  • You're self-employed — your tax returns show low income due to business deductions
  • You own 5+ properties — conventional lenders get nervous above 4-10 mortgages
  • You want to scale fast — no income documentation means faster, simpler closings
  • You're buying in an LLC — DSCR loans can close directly in your LLC's name
  • Your DTI is maxed out — DSCR doesn't care about your other debts

Pros and Cons of DSCR Loans

Pros

  • No personal income verification required
  • Unlimited number of financed properties
  • Can close in LLC or entity name
  • Faster closing than conventional
  • Available for short-term rental income (Airbnb)

Cons

  • Higher interest rates (1-2% above conventional)
  • Larger down payment required (20-25%)
  • Prepayment penalties (typically 3-5 years)
  • Higher origination fees
  • Property must generate sufficient rental income

How to Get the Best DSCR Loan Rate

  1. Improve your credit score — 740+ gets the best pricing
  2. Put 25%+ down — lower LTV = lower rate
  3. Choose properties with high DSCR — 1.25+ gets better terms
  4. Shop multiple lenders — DSCR rates vary significantly between lenders
  5. Consider rate buydowns — paying points upfront can lower your rate
  6. Use our Property Analyzer to verify rental income estimates before applying

Key Takeaways

  • DSCR loans qualify you based on property income, not personal income
  • Most lenders require a DSCR of 1.0-1.25 with 20-25% down
  • Perfect for self-employed investors and those scaling beyond conventional loan limits
  • Expect rates 1-2% higher than conventional with 3-5 year prepayment penalties
  • Compare DSCR with hard money loans to find the right financing for each deal
