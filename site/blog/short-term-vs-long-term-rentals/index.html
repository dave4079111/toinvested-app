Short-Term vs Long-Term Rentals: Which Is More Profitable?

By David J. Moore February 5, 2026 9 min read

The short-term rental revolution (Airbnb, VRBO) has created a new debate for investors: should you rent nightly for premium rates, or stick with traditional long-term tenants? The answer depends on your market, risk tolerance, and how involved you want to be.

Income Comparison

Same Property, Different Strategies

Property: 3BR/2BA near downtown, $300,000 purchase price

Long-term rental: $2,000/month = $24,000/year

Short-term rental (65% occupancy, $175/night): 237 nights × $175 = $41,475/year

Income premium: STR generates 73% more gross income

But gross income tells only part of the story. Short-term rentals come with significantly higher expenses.

STR net advantage: $4,264/year (30% more) — but with 5x the management effort

Regulations: The STR Wild Card

The biggest risk with short-term rentals is regulatory change. Cities across the country are restricting or banning STRs:

  • License/permit requirements — annual fees of $100-$2,000+
  • Occupancy limits — maximum nights per year (e.g., 90 days in some cities)
  • Zoning restrictions — STRs banned in residential zones
  • HOA prohibitions — many HOAs and condos ban short-term rentals
  • Tax obligations — occupancy taxes (hotel taxes) of 5-15%

Always verify local STR regulations before purchasing a property for short-term rental use.

Management Intensity

Long-Term Rental Management

  • Find tenant once per 1-2 years
  • Collect rent monthly (automated)
  • Handle occasional maintenance requests
  • Time commitment: 2-4 hours/month

Short-Term Rental Management

  • Manage bookings and guest communication daily
  • Coordinate cleanings between guests (sometimes same-day turnovers)
  • Handle guest issues, reviews, and pricing optimization
  • Restock supplies, replace worn items regularly
  • Time commitment: 15-25 hours/month (or 20-30% management fee)

The Hybrid Approach: Medium-Term Rentals

Medium-term rentals (30+ day stays) combine benefits of both:

  • Higher rates than LTR — furnished monthly stays command 30-50% premium
  • Less turnover than STR — fewer cleanings, less wear
  • Fewer regulations — 30+ day stays usually avoid STR restrictions
  • Target markets: traveling nurses, corporate relocations, insurance claims, digital nomads

Which Strategy Should You Choose?

Choose Long-Term If...Choose Short-Term If...
You want hands-off investingYou enjoy hospitality and guest interaction
Your market has strict STR regulationsYou're in a tourist or high-demand market
You value income stabilityYou can handle income seasonality
You don't want to furnish the propertyYou're willing to invest in furnishing
You have multiple properties to manageYou have 1-3 properties and time to manage

Key Takeaways

  • STRs generate 30-70% more gross income but have 50-80% higher expenses
  • After expenses, STRs typically net 20-40% more — but require 5x the management
  • Regulatory risk is the #1 threat to STR profitability — always verify local laws
  • Medium-term rentals (30+ days) offer a hybrid approach with less regulation and management
  • Choose based on your market, time availability, and management preference
