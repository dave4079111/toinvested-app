6 Loan Types Every Investor Should Know

Each financing tool solves a different problem. Match the right loan to your strategy.

🏦

Hard Money Loans

Short-term, asset-based loans for fix-and-flip and value-add projects. Quick closings, higher rates, based on property value not income.

9-14% Rate 6-24 Months 65-75% LTV
📈

DSCR Loans

Long-term rental loans qualified on property cash flow, not personal income. Perfect for scaling a portfolio without W-2 verification.

7-9% Rate 30-Year Term 75-80% LTV
🌃

Bridge Loans

Short-term financing to "bridge" between purchase and permanent financing or sale. Ideal when you need to act fast on time-sensitive deals.

8-12% Rate 6-36 Months 70-80% LTV
🤝

Private Money

Loans from individuals, not institutions. Flexible terms negotiated directly. Build relationships with private lenders for repeat deal funding.

8-15% Rate Flexible Terms Negotiable LTV
🏗

Construction Loans

Fund ground-up builds or major renovations with draw-based financing. Lender releases funds in stages as construction milestones are met.

10-14% Rate 12-18 Months Up to 90% LTC
🏢

Mezzanine Financing

Subordinate debt that sits between the senior loan and equity. Used in commercial deals to increase leverage beyond what a primary lender offers.

12-20% Rate 1-5 Years 75-90% LTC

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the typical interest rate for a hard money loan? ▼
Hard money loan rates typically range from 9% to 14% depending on the lender, borrower experience, loan-to-value ratio, and property type. Most lenders also charge 1-4 origination points upfront.
How fast can a hard money loan close? ▼
Hard money loans can close in as fast as 5-14 business days, compared to 30-45 days for conventional financing. Some lenders offer same-week closings for experienced borrowers with complete documentation.
What is the difference between hard money and DSCR loans? ▼
Hard money loans are short-term (6-24 months) and asset-based, ideal for fix-and-flip or bridge scenarios. DSCR loans are long-term (30 years) rental property loans that qualify based on the property's rental income rather than borrower income. DSCR loans typically have lower rates (7-9%) compared to hard money (9-14%).
Do I need good credit for a hard money loan? ▼
Hard money lenders focus primarily on the property value and deal quality rather than borrower credit. While some lenders have minimum credit score requirements (often 600-650), the primary qualification is the property's after-repair value (ARV) and loan-to-value ratio. Poor credit may result in higher rates or points.
What is a DSCR loan and how does it work? ▼
A DSCR (Debt Service Coverage Ratio) loan qualifies borrowers based on the property's rental income relative to the loan payment. A DSCR of 1.0 means rent equals the mortgage payment. Most lenders require a DSCR of 1.0-1.25. These loans don't require personal income verification, making them ideal for self-employed investors or those with complex tax returns.
Can I use hard money for my first deal? ▼
Yes, many hard money lenders work with first-time investors. However, expect higher rates (12-14%) and lower leverage (65-70% LTV) as a new borrower. Some lenders like Groundfloor and Kiavi have programs specifically designed for newer investors. Having a solid deal, contractor team, and exit strategy will strengthen your application significantly.
